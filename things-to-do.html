Things to Do on Mars.

Imagine the possibilities on this beautiful planet! Mars has so, so many things to offer with its diverse landscapes stretching from pole to pole, from massive canyons towering multiple Earth's mountains to vast deserts, giant craters and ancient volcanoes that leave even the most avid world traveller in a trance. The scenic beauty this extraterrestrial world has to offer will make any Earth's wonder green with envy.

Below are just a few of the many activities you can do on Mars. Whether you're an adrenaline junkie looking for the next thrill, a nature lover seeking the most breathtaking views, or a history buff eager to explore the remnants of the past, Mars has something for everyone. Step on the Red Planet and breathe in the experience of a lifetime!

Grand Canyon of Mars Tour

Explore Valles Marineris, a canyon so massive it spans almost the width of Canada from coast to coast! This extraordinary terrain is a must-see for any visitor up for a thrilling exploration.

Olympus Mons Hike

Embark on a journey to the tallest volcano in the Solar System! Sitting at an incredible 22-kilometre high, Olympus Mons towers almost three times our Mount Everest. With Mars' lower gravity, this hike, despite long, is not as strenuous as it sounds. At the summit, you'll be rewarded with a breathtaking view of the Martian landscape.

Rover Racing

Test your racing skills on the dusty racetracks! Rent a rover and compete against skilled racers in this thrilling sport. Navigate through rugged red terrain while dodging ancient dunes and crater rims. A tournament is rumoured to be held soon, so start practising now!

Dust Surfing

Chase the thrill and surf in dust storms! These storms are common, and every few years they engulf the whole planet, making this a rare opportunity to experience the absolute adrenaline rush while surfing through massive sand dunes and fully immerse yourself in the powerful winds. A once-in-a-lifetime opportunity for those seeking the extremest of adventures.

Moongazing

Witness the spectacular twin moons of Mars! Phobos and Deimos, the potato-shaped moons, orbit much closer to the planet than our Moon to Earth. Their irregular shapes and close proximity make them a fascinating sight to behold. Join a stargazing tour to learn more about these moons and the Martian sky, and the inevitable demise of Phobos as it spirals closer to the planet.

North Polar Cap Expedition

Calling brave explorers! Venture to the icy North Polar Cap of Mars and witness the breathtaking beauty of frozen desserts - a stark white constrast to the rusted red everywhere else. Yes, Mars has ice (real water ice, pssst), indicating the possibility of ancient life. Who knows, you could be the next pioneer to unravel one of the biggest mysteries scientists have been on the hunt for!
